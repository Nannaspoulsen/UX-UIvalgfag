Japanese ingredients set to transform your cooking

When I traveled through Japan, I was struck by how simple yet powerful the food really is. From sushi and onigiri to ramen and fried chicken — it’s all about balance, comfort, and bold flavors. What surprised me most was how many of the essentials are things we already know: soy sauce, sesame oil, miso… even ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, and curry powder sneak their way into Japanese kitchens.

How to make the perfect pasta!

Italy has always felt like a second home to me. The way they treat pasta is more than cooking — it’s culture, tradition, and love on a plate. I spent my time there learning how simple ingredients like flour, eggs, tomatoes, and olive oil can become something extraordinary when treated with respect. From silky tagliatelle to rustic orecchiette, here are the recipes that capture the soul of Italy — simple, heartfelt, and made to be shared.
